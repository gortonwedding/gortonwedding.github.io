Alicia & Acton

Acton and Alicia are currently students at the University of Illinois.

Alicia is a licensed nurse and is working on her BA in Psychology.

Acton is a data journalist and a PhD candidate in Informatics, hoping to finish his research within the year.

How we Met

Acton likes to emphasize we met due to a classified ad posted to Craigslist.com in 2010.

Technically, that's true... but it wasn't nearly as scandalous as that sounds.

It was the ROOMMATE section.

We just lucked out.

Acton was searching for a new roommate at the time Alicia was moving back to Champaign.

We knew each other for nearly a year, and our friendship grew as we found ourselves enjoying each other's company more and more.

We decided to give things a chance, and we fell in love.

The Proposal

Our fifth anniversary was coming up on New Years Eve and we wanted to do something special. After searching a few locations, we decided on Chicago and found an event called Chi-town Rising. Acton’s friend Boyang was playing with his band at the event, so we decided to go.

It was a cold evening but there was a sense of exhilaration in the air as we walked along the Chicago River. When midnight hit, fireworks began bursting all around us, and we stood along the Chicago river watching the sky.

Under the fireworks, with snow falling, Acton proposed. He of course put his spin on it, using a trick to reveal a hidden message in a photograph of us that read: “Will you Marry me?”

The Wedding Destination

Dear Family and Friends,

We are very excited to be celebrating our wedding in the Dominican Republic. It has always been our dream to have a beach wedding, in a warm tropical place, surrounded by our family and friends. We have chosen the Dominican Republic based on recommendations by friends, family and a lot of research. It is very important for us that everyone feels invited and can join us. We realize how much is being asked to travel so far and we hope the plan we’ve put together helps.

Punta Cana was chosen because it struck the right balance between natural beauty, ease of travel, and affordability. The location promises endless activities and is near enough features to allow for plenty of exploration to see the island beyond the resort.

Dreams Palms Beach Resort and Spa is a four star “all-inclusive” hotel recommended by family members that have previously stayed here, our wedding planner, and a number of review websites. We believe the hotel offers the best value for everyone considering the myriad of challenges that naturally come with planning an event of this nature.

Many of the hotels and resorts in the Punta Cana area require weddings to book a minimum of a seven night stay, but the Dreams Palm Beach Resort requires a three day minimum. You can choose to stay longer, of course, because we’re trying to give you every excuse to trade the frigid January winters for white sandy beaches and turquoise colored water.

Are you hooked yet?

Good, because we are planning on being there from January 3rd to the 9th, 2017.

To help make your decision and planning easier, we are working to ensure that all you have to do is show up. The meals and many of the activities are all included with the room. The resort houses five restaurants, each with a different flavor and friendly to dietary requests.

We have secured discounts for travel and hotel bookings through a dedicated travel agent we’ve hired to help everyone find the best accommodations whether you choose to stay at the hotel or not, or need to arrange for different travel options. Of course you are always welcome to make your own arrangements, but the travel agent is already paid for and is expecting your call.

Many of our friends and family prefer to seek their own adventures, and that may mean wanting to explore the island or stay at different lodgings. There are many wonderful places throughout Punta Cana and the Dominican Republic at large, but if you choose to stay outside of the hotel, a day pass will need to be purchased for each person. There are different day passes which include meals and activities; more about that towards the bottom.

Above all else, we want you with us, so don’t worry about spending anything extra on wedding gifts and treat this like a vacation. We’ll have meeting times setup for everyone to come together, but don’t feel required to attend any of them. We’ve tried to also include enough familiar faces in the invitation list so that everyone has a friend or family to be with and won’t feel like they’re being ignored or forced to fit in with a bunch of strangers.

We have hired LUXE to coordinate our wedding planning and they will be responsible for all hotel room bookings and air-fare. We have already reserved a block of rooms at the resort but we’re worried that once they are filled it will be difficult to make reservations at the hotel. We have arranged for as much privacy and exclusivity as we can for the wedding, but this is a popular destination and it will be a popular time of year for traveling to the tropics. Every room at the resort will be booked by someone, so we are working quickly and diligently through the planning to ensure everyone is given a head start to make their plans and reservations.

We are planning for the ceremony to begin at 5pm on January 7th, 2017.
